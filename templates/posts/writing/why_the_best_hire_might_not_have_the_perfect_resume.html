Why the best hire might not have the perfect resume.

Instruction

Watching & Reading(25 min)

We will watch Why the best hire might not have the perfect resume together. After watching, you have more time to rewatch the video or read the transcript on your own. Do not hesitate to use a dictionary. Do not rush. If you cannot read it in 25 minutes, that's OK. It's better to understand a few paragraphs completely than to understand the whole article only vaguely.

Answer the following questions as you read.

  • Identify the subject(主語) and the verb(動詞) of each clause(節).
  • For each article(冠詞) or lack of thereof, think about why.

Whenever you have trouble understanding a sentence or part of a sentence, ask me.

Short lecture(5 min)

I will go over the sentences, phrases, or words that many of you had trouble with. I will also touch on more advanced grammar appeared in the article that I did not cover in English Seminar 2-7.

Writing(40 min)

Answer the following questions. If you don't have time, you don't have to answer all of them. Please type it in a word processor.

  1. Summarise the talk in one or two sentences.
  2. Do you have some experiences that might not look good on resume?
  3. Describe what "Scrapper" is.
  4. Do you agree with her hiring criteria? Why or why not?
  5. Write general comments about the article.

Feedback and Correction(15 min)

I will give feedback and correct grammatical mistakes on your writing.

Email your work to kenzotakahashi2@gmail.com . I will compile all of your work and share it with you so that you can read your classmates' work. If you don't want to share a particular answer, write (private) at the beginning of the answer. But please share as many of them as possible.
