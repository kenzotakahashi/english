Poor and Traumatized at Harvard

Instruction

Reading(25 min)

Read the first 9 paragraphs of Poor and Traumatized at Harvard(the last paragraph ends with "I didn't want anyone to know I was falling apart."). There are many difficult words in this article, so do not hesitate to use a dictionary. Do not rush. If you cannot read it in 25 minutes, that's OK. It's better to understand a few paragraphs completely than to understand the whole article only vaguely. If you finish reading it, keep reading the rest of the article and even the responses.

As far as I know, there are 19 adjective clauses(形容詞節) and 8 noun clauses(名詞節) in the article. That is a lot. If you don't understand them, it will be really tough to read.

Answer the following questions as you read.

  • Identify the subject(主語) and the verb(動詞) of each clause(節).
  • For each article(冠詞) or lack of thereof, think about why.

Whenever you have trouble understanding a sentence or part of a sentence, ask me.

Short lecture(5 min)

I will go over the sentences, phrases, or words that many of you had trouble with. I will also touch on more advanced grammar appeared in the article that I did not cover in English Seminar 2-7.

Writing(40 min)

Answer the following questions. If you don't have time, you don't have to answer all of them. Please type it in a word processor.

  1. Harvard is considered to be the best college in the world. Then why was the author miserable and unhappy at Harvard? Summarize it.
  2. Did reading this article change your criteria for choosing a college? Explain.
  3. If you were accepted to Harvard and only had to pay the same tuition as the colleges you are considering right now, would you go to Harvard? Why or why not?
  4. Have you ever had a similar experience as the author? That is, have you ever had difficulty in finding a community or friends that understand you?
  5. Write general comments about the article. Read the responses at the end for examples.

Feedback and Correction(15 min)

I will give feedback and correct grammatical mistakes on your writing.

Email your work to kenzotakahashi2@gmail.com . I will compile all of your work and share it with you so that you can read your classmates' work. If you don't want to share a particular answer, write (private) at the beginning of the answer. But please share as many of them as possible.
