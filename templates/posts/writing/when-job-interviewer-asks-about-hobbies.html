What hiring managers really want to know when they ask, 'What are your hobbies?'

Instruction

Reading(25 min)

Read What hiring managers really want to know when they ask, 'What are your hobbies?'. Do not hesitate to use a dictionary. Do not rush. If you cannot read it in 25 minutes, that's OK. It's better to understand a few paragraphs completely than to understand the whole article only vaguely. If you finish reading it, keep reading the rest of the article and even the responses.

Answer the following questions as you read.

  • Identify the subject(主語) and the verb(動詞) of each clause(節).
  • For each article(冠詞) or lack of thereof, think about why.

Whenever you have trouble understanding a sentence or part of a sentence, ask me.

Short lecture(5 min)

I will go over the sentences, phrases, or words that many of you had trouble with. I will also touch on more advanced grammar appeared in the article that I did not cover in English Seminar 2-7.

Writing(40 min)

Answer the following questions. If you don't have time, you don't have to answer all of them. Please type it in a word processor.

  1. Which section(s) was the most helpful? Why?
  2. Which section(s), if any, do you disagree with? Why?
  3. Answer the question 'What are your hobbies?' as if you were at a job interview. Make sure you meet all the 9 points in the article(unless you disagree with some of them). Since this is writing and not conversation, you can't just say a few words and wait for a manager to ask you a question. Think about what follow up questions the maneger would ask and address them in your writing.
  4. Write general comments about the article.

Feedback and Correction(15 min)

I will give feedback and correct grammatical mistakes on your writing.

Email your work to kenzotakahashi2@gmail.com . I will compile all of your work and share it with you so that you can read your classmates' work. If you don't want to share a particular answer, write (private) at the beginning of the answer. But please share as many of them as possible.
